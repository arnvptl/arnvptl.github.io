About Arun Bhatia

Ex-IAS officer, dedicated to fighting corruption in Indian bureaucracy.

This bio-data is a three decade long story of attempting to enforce honesty in a corrupt third world bureaucracy. Almost from the moment of joining service (IAS) it became clear that the real enemy was the corrupt political establishment in India.

Know Me - Arun Bhatia

My Journey in the Indian Administrative Service

With over three decades of experience in the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), I have witnessed firsthand the challenges and complexities of public administration in India. My career has been defined by an unwavering commitment to integrity and transparency in government service.

Early Career and Challenges

From the very beginning of my service, it became evident that the greatest challenge was not the complexity of administrative work, but the pervasive corruption within the system. The pressure to compromise on principles was constant, coming from political establishments and corrupt colleagues alike.

Fighting Corruption

Throughout my career, I made it my mission to expose and fight against corruption at every level. This often put me at odds with powerful interests, resulting in difficult postings, threats, and professional challenges. However, I remained steadfast in my commitment to serve the people honestly.

Lessons Learned

My experiences have taught me that fighting corruption requires not just individual courage, but systemic changes in how we approach public service. It requires transparency, accountability, and a collective commitment to the public good over personal gain.

Current Mission

Today, I continue my fight against corruption through writing, speaking, and mentoring young civil servants. My goal is to inspire a new generation of public servants who will choose integrity over convenience and public service over personal profit.

About the Book

My book "Fight Against Corruption: An IAS Officer's Journey" is a detailed account of my three decades in the Indian Administrative Service and my continuous battle against corruption in the Indian bureaucracy.

What You'll Find

  • Real-life case studies of corruption in government
  • Strategies for maintaining integrity in challenging environments
  • Analysis of systemic issues in Indian bureaucracy
  • Recommendations for administrative reforms
  • Personal anecdotes and experiences from the field

Target Audience

This book is essential reading for civil servants, policy makers, students of public administration, and anyone interested in understanding the challenges of governance in developing countries.
